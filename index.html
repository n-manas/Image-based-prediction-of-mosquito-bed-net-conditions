Image-based prediction of mosquito bed net conditions

Center for Medical Image Analysis & Navigation
Department of Biomedical Engineering
University of Basel

*This project is part of the BRCCH-funded ViALLIN project and is run in collaboration with

Description

Background

Malaria is a life-threatening disease that is transmitted by Anopheles mosquitoes. It is among the top 10 causes of death in low-income countries, with hundreds of thousands of deaths occurring every year. Out of the current approaches to prevent malaria, the use of long-lasting insecticidal nets (LLINs) has proven to be an effective, low-cost method to prevent parasite transmission, reducing mortality caused by this disease. However, the protective action of LLINs strongly relies on their physical integrity, making monitoring their condition and fast replacement essential. Currently, the bed net condition is surveilled employing the WHO standard, which assesses fabric integrity by counting the number of holes and tears in a net, classifying them according to their diameter’s size. However, this technique is time-consuming and does not accurately reflect the total hole area. Thus, a fast, reliable method is needed to calculate this parameter to adequately plan bed net distribution campaigns in malaria-endemic countries.

Objectives

The aim of this project is to develop a fast, simple and reliable alternative to estimate current and future bed net conditions from images using Deep Learning-based methods.

Relevance

The resulting models will provide a faster and more reliable approach to assess LLIN condition, thus enabling national programs to effectively plan the mass-distribution of these nets to populations at risk, contributing to the protection of millions of people.

Overview of our piepline. For data collection, bed nets are spanned over a frame with a black cloth as background and all five sides are photographed. Then, using Deep Learning models we extract defect and frame size information to compute the hole surface area. This enables the automatic, fast monitoring of bed net condition.

To generate the ground truth data, a Graphical User Interface was developed using PyQT5.
